# Temporal q_cal profile-inference v4: post-open development protocol

Date frozen: 2026-07-22

## Question

The v3 audit found that the one-step plug-in estimator under-covered and that
iterative affine variable projection reduced median `q_cal` error by only 8.91%,
below its registered 10% gate. This v4 experiment asks a narrower question:

> For the *same iterative point estimate*, is the remaining under-coverage
> mainly caused by the covariance approximation, or by centre bias from ridge
> absorption, weak identification, or forward-model mismatch?

This is a post-open diagnostic. It is not a learned operator, a new algorithm,
a real BOST reconstruction, or evidence of generalisation.

## Frozen data slice

- Reuse the three already opened synthetic rigs and their three already opened
  random directions from v3. No fresh-rig claim is allowed.
- Test one null cell per rig (`q=0`) and all three directions at `q/q_ref=1`
  and `q/q_ref=2`: 21 cells total.
- Use the v3 low-noise level `noise multiplier = 1/128`.
- Generate 500 new deterministic noise replicates per cell under the v4 seed
  namespace. Replicates 0--249 are the calibration half; 250--499 are the
  internal evaluation half. This split is frozen before results are opened.
- The nonlinear clean observation is generated by the synthetic voxel forward;
  estimation still uses the local affine forward. The mismatch is retained.

The 10,500 observations are repeated noise realisations conditional on only
three rigs. They are not 10,500 independent physical experiments.

## Point-estimator arms

All arms receive the same observation, ridge coefficient, local ball, affine
components, and numerical tolerances.

1. `P0 one_step_plugin`: v3 plug-in point estimator and native covariance proxy.
2. `P1 one_step_full_profile`: exactly the first accepted full-profile
   Gauss--Newton/trust-region update from `q=0`.
3. `P2 iterative_full_profile`: continue the same P1 path to the frozen v3
   iteration limit. P1 is a strict prefix of P2.
4. `P3 corrected_two_fold_crossfit`: fixed even/odd-frame nuisance fits with
   first-order absorption correction. This is an ablation/negative control,
   not a candidate.

Point error, field error, sequence error, boundary hits, objective monotonicity,
factorisations, profile evaluations, Jacobian solves, and wall time stay in the
ledger. A failed or boundary result remains in the denominator.

## Uncertainty arms at the same P2 endpoint

Let `r=y-Bx`, `s_k=dx/dq_k`, `d_j=B_j x`, and `c_k=d_k+B s_k`. Define

```text
G_jk = c_j^T c_k + lambda s_j^T s_k
A_jk = d_j^T c_k - r^T B_j s_k
M_jk = sigma^2 c_j^T c_k
```

The registered primary uncertainty arm is:

```text
M1 iterative_exact_godambe: V = A^-1 M A^-T
```

The secondary arms are:

```text
M0 plugin_native:             V = sigma^2 (C_plugin^T C_plugin)^-1
M2 iterative_gn_sandwich:    V = G^-1 M G^-1
M3 penalized_profile_contrast:
   T = 2 [F_profile(q_true)-F_profile(q_hat)] / sigma^2
M4 corrected_crossfit_wald:   fixed two-fold diagnostic covariance
```

`M3` is deliberately not called a likelihood-ratio statistic: the profiled
objective contains deterministic Tikhonov penalty rows. A chi-square cutoff is
reported only as a diagnostic reference.

## Calibration without a false fresh claim

For each method and calibration cell, select the finite-sample order statistic

```text
k = ceil((n_cal + 1) * 0.95), n_cal = 250.
```

The development envelope is the maximum kth order statistic across all 21
opened cells. It is then applied unchanged to the 250 evaluation replicates in
each cell. This can reveal whether a single conservative threshold transfers
across new noise draws in the same opened rigs. It cannot establish new-rig,
new-camera, experimental, or BOST coverage.

## Fail-closed checks

- Analytic profile score bread `A` must match centred finite differences in `q`.
- Observation derivative `D` must match centred finite differences in `y`.
- Covariance matrices must be finite, symmetric, positive semidefinite, and
  rank 5/5 under the frozen `1e-9` relative threshold.
- Boundary hits, non-monotone objectives, negative profile contrasts beyond
  numerical tolerance, and numerical exceptions remain in method denominators.
- Coverage is always paired with maximum ellipsoid-axis radius. Wider regions
  cannot be described as a precision improvement.
- Cross-fitting is not treated as independent-data inference because all folds
  share the same three-dimensional field, geometry, and transport.
- Truth-based q/field/sequence metrics are evaluator-only and never feed an
  estimator or threshold.

## Development gates

The primary `M1` diagnostic is informative only if all of the following hold on
the internal evaluation half:

- every cell has empirical coverage at least 0.90;
- pooled empirical coverage is at least 0.925 and its two-sided 95%
  Clopper--Pearson interval contains 0.95;
- calibrated maximum-axis radius has median at most `0.5 q_ref` and p90 at most
  `q_ref`;
- no numerical failures and no false exclusion hidden by abstention;
- P2 field/sequence median error is not more than 2% worse than P0.

Passing these gates would produce only
`POSTOPEN_SAME_RIG_NOISE_SPLIT_SIGNAL`. Failing any gate produces
`POSTOPEN_DEVELOPMENT_FORENSICS_NO_GO`.

## Fresh audit locked behind this experiment

No fresh audit is run in this module. A later protocol must be committed before
opening results and must use new rig/field/camera/transport seeds, `q=0`, the ten
signed coordinate axes, signed operator-only anchor weak directions, independent
noise calibration, and at least 1,000 primary replicates per cell. The primary
method may not be changed after that manifest is frozen.

  https://doi.org/10.1080/01621459.2000.10474219

  https://doi.org/10.1111/ectj.12097

  https://doi.org/10.1214/aoms/1177705693
- White (1982), covariance under model misspecification:
  https://doi.org/10.2307/1912526

These sources motivate optimisation and inference diagnostics. None supplies a
finite-sample coverage theorem for high-dimensional ridge-regularised BOST.
